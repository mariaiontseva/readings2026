I bow (vande) to the blessed guru (śrīgurum), supreme bliss (paramānandam), embodiment of own-bliss (svānandavigraham), by whose (yasya) mere presence (sānnidhyamātreṇa) the body (tanuḥ) becomes consciousness-bliss (cidānandāyate).
I venerate the glorious guru, who is supreme bliss, who is the manifestation of his own bliss, merely by being in whose presence one's body becomes consciousness and bliss.

If he performs a controlled inhalation through the left channel (iḍā) and again exhales through the other channel, then inhales through the right channel (piṅgalā), holds it sufficiently long, releases it through the left [channel] and meditates on the two orbs of the sun and moon in this way, all the ascetic's channels are purified after three months.
