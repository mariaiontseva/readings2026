C1OPENING INVOCATION - MAṄGALA VERSES
1
hitārtham akhilam yena
brahmādikam jagat sṛṣṭam
praṇamya tam paśupatim
śirasā sadasatpatim
Translation
For the sake of (artham) welfare (hita), the whole (akhilam) world (jagat) beginning with (ādikam) Brahmā (brahma) [was] created (sṛṣṭam) by whom (yena), having bowed (praṇamya) to that (tam) Lord of Souls (paśupatim) with the head (śirasā), [the] Lord (patim) of existent (sat) [and] nonexistent (asat)...
Having bowed my head to that Lord of existent and nonexistent, Lord of Souls (Paśupati), by whom the whole world has been emitted from Brahmā down, for the sake of (our) salvation...
Словарь
• hita-artham = for the sake of welfare/salvation (acc. sg. n.)
• akhilam = whole, entire (nom. sg. n.)
• yena = by whom (instr. sg. m.)
• brahma-ādikam = beginning with Brahmā (nom. sg. n.)
• jagat = world, universe (nom. sg. n.)
• sṛṣṭam = created, emitted (nom. sg. n. PPP from √sṛj)
• praṇamya = having bowed (abs. from pra-√nam)
• tam = that, him (acc. sg. m.)
• paśu-patim = Lord of Souls (acc. sg. m.)
• śirasā = with the head (instr. sg. n.)
• sat-asat-patim = Lord of existent and nonexistent (acc. sg. m.)
Объяснение
Opening invocatory sentence. Absolutive praṇamya = reverential gesture completed before main action. Instrumental śirasā = prostration with head (highest respect). Paśu-pati = "Lord of bound souls/animals" (technical term). Sat-asat-pati = Lord of manifest (sat) and unmanifest (asat) realities. Relative clause yena...sṛṣṭam describes Paśupati as cosmic creator. Compound hita-artha = "welfare-purpose" (soteriological aim). Brahma-ādika = "Brahmā-and-so-on" (the entire hierarchy from highest deity down). PPP sṛṣṭa from √sṛj = divine creative act of emission/projection.
2
arthātiśayasaṃpannam
jñānātiśayam uttamam
pañcārtham saṃpravakṣyāmi
yathāvad anupūrvaśaḥ
Translation
[Which is] imbued with (saṃpannam) superior (atiśaya) significance (artha), superior (atiśaya) in knowledge (jñāna), [and] most excellent (uttamam), the Five Realities (pañcārtham) I will teach (saṃpravakṣyāmi) duly (yathāvat), in proper order (anupūrvaśaḥ).
I will duly teach, in good order and adhering to the truth, 'The Five Realities' (Pañcārtha), which is imbued with superior significance, is superior in knowledge, and most excellent.
Словарь
• artha-atiśaya-saṃpannam = imbued with superior significance (acc. sg. n.)
• jñāna-atiśayam = superior in knowledge (acc. sg. n.)
• uttamam = most excellent, supreme (acc. sg. n.)
• pañca-artham = the Five Realities (acc. sg. n.)
• saṃpravakṣyāmi = I will teach (1st sg. fut. from sam-pra-√vac)
• yathā-vat = duly, according to truth (adv.)
• anupūrvaśaḥ = in proper order, sequentially (adv.)
Объяснение
Main declarative statement with compound object. Main verb: saṃpravakṣyāmi (future tense = authorial intention). Pañca-artha = technical term for the five ontological categories (kārya, kāraṇa, yoga, vidhi, duḥkhānta). Three qualifications of the teaching: artha-atiśaya-saṃpanna = "filled/endowed with (saṃpanna) surpassing (atiśaya) meaning/significance"; jñāna-atiśaya = extraordinary in knowledge content; uttama = superlative - highest among teachings. Yathāvat = "according to reality/truth" (accurate transmission). Anupūrvaśas = "in sequence without skipping" (systematic pedagogy).
¶
Full Translation of Opening Verses:

"Having bowed my head to that Lord of existent and nonexistent, Lord of Souls (Paśupati), by whom the whole world has been emitted from Brahmā down, for the sake of (our) salvation, I will duly teach, in good order and adhering to the truth, 'The Five Realities' (Pañcārtha), which is imbued with superior significance, is superior in knowledge, and most excellent."

C3PĀŚUPATA SŪTRA 1.1 - THE OPENING APHORISM
1
athātaḥ paśupateḥ pāśupataṃ yogavidhim vyākhyāsyāmaḥ
Translation
Certainly (atha), therefore (ataḥ), [regarding] Paśupati (paśupateḥ), we shall explain (vyākhyāsyāmaḥ) the Pāśupata (pāśupatam) praxis (vidhim) of union (yoga).
Certainly, therefore, of Paśupati, we shall explain the Pāśupata praxis of union.
Словарь
• atha = certainly, now (particle)
• ataḥ = therefore, because of this (particle)
• paśu-pateḥ = of Paśupati, Lord of Souls (gen. sg. m.)
• pāśupatam = Pāśupata, relating to Paśupati (acc. sg. n.)
• yoga-vidhim = praxis/method of union (acc. sg. m.)
• vi-ā-khy-āsy-āmaḥ = we shall explain (1st pl. fut. from vi-ā-√khyā)
Объяснение
The foundational sūtra. Two particles (atha/ataḥ) establish authority and causality. Genitive paśupateḥ = genitive of reference/relation. Pāśupata = taddhita derivative (that which pertains to/is proclaimed by Paśupati). Yoga-vidhi = compound "union-method" (systematic practice). Future vyākhyāsyāmaḥ = commitment to exposition.
¶
Full Translation of PS 1.1:

"Certainly, therefore, of Paśupati, we shall explain the Pāśupata praxis of union."
C4KAUṆḌINYA'S COMMENTARY - WORD DIVISION
1
etat prathamasūtram śāstrādāv uccāryate
Translation
This (etat) first (prathama) sūtra (sūtram) is spoken (uccāryate) at the beginning (ādau) of the śāstra (śāstra).
This first Sūtra is spoken at the beginning of the Śāstra.
Словарь
• etat = this (nom. sg. n.)
• prathama-sūtram = first sūtra (nom. sg. n.)
• śāstra-ādau = at the beginning of the śāstra (loc. sg. m.)
• uccāryate = is spoken (3rd sg. pres. pass. from ud-√car)
Объяснение
Establishes textual location. Demonstrative etat points to PS 1.1. Compound śāstra-āda = "beginning of the teaching". Passive uccāryate = traditional recitation/pronunciation.
2
tadanantaram padavigr ahaḥ kriyate
Translation
Immediately (anantaram) after that (tat), a division (vigrahaḥ) of words (pada) is made (kriyate).
Next a division is made of the words.
Словарь
• tat-anantaram = immediately after that (adv.)
• pada-vigrahaḥ = word-division, analysis (nom. sg. m.)
• kriyate = is made (3rd sg. pres. pass. from √kṛ)
Объяснение
Methodological statement. Compound pada-vigraha = "word-separation/analysis" (grammatical procedure). Tadanantaram = temporal sequence. Passive kriyate = standard commentarial practice.
3
aṣṭapadam sūtram
Translation
The sūtra (sūtram) [consists of] eight (aṣṭa) words (padam).
The Sūtra counts eight words.
Словарь
• aṣṭa-padam = eight-worded (nom. sg. n.)
• sūtram = the sūtra (nom. sg. n.)
Объяснение
Numerical analysis. Bahuvṛḥi compound aṣṭa-pada = "having eight words/padas". Establishes precise count for grammatical parsing.
4
tatra athāta iti dve pade naipātike
Translation
Therein (tatra), the two (dve) words (pade) 'atha' (certainly) and 'ataḥ' (therefore) [are] particles (naipātike).
The two words atha ('certainly') and ataḥ ('because of') are particles (nipāta).
Словарь
• tatra = therein, among these (adv.)
• atha-ataḥ = atha and ataḥ (indecl.)
• iti = thus
• dve = two (nom. dual n.)
• pade = words (nom. dual n.)
• naipātike = particles (nom. dual n.)
Объяснение
Grammatical classification. Naipātika = indeclinable particles (from nipāta). Dual number (dve pade) for the two function words. These establish logical framework (certainty + causation) without inflectional meaning.
5
paśupater ity etat padam parigraha arthonocc āryate
Translation
This (etat) word (padam) 'paśupateḥ' (of Paśupati) is spoken (uccāryate) for the purpose (arthena) of acceptance (parigraha).
Paśupater ('of Paśupati'), this word is spoken to indicate acceptance (parigraha).
Словарь
• paśu-pateḥ = of Paśupati (gen. sg.)
• iti = thus
• etat = this (nom. sg. n.)
• padam = word (nom. sg. n.)
• parigraha-arthena = for the purpose of acceptance/inclusion (instr. sg. m.)
• uccāryate = is spoken (3rd sg. pres. pass.)
Объяснение
Functional analysis. Parigraha-artha = "purpose of taking up/embracing" - indicates acceptance/adoption of Paśupati's teaching. Genitive case shows relation/reference to the divine source.
6
pāśupatam iti taddhitam
Translation
'Pāśupatam' (pāśupatam) [is] a nominal derivative (taddhitam).
'Pāśupata' is a nominal derivative (taddhita).
Словарь
• pāśupatam = Pāśupata (nom. sg. n.)
• iti = thus
• taddhitam = nominal derivative (nom. sg. n.)
Объяснение
Morphological classification. Taddhita = secondary nominal suffix (Pāṇinian term for derived nominals). Formed from paśu-pati + suffix -a, meaning "that which relates to/belongs to Paśupati."
7
yogavidhim iti sāmāsikam
Translation
'Yogavidhim' (yogavidhim) (praxis of union) [is] a compound (sāmāsikam).
'Praxis of union' is a compound (samāsa).
Словарь
• yoga-vidhim = praxis of union (acc. sg. m.)
• iti = thus
• sāmāsikam = compound (nom. sg. n.)
Объяснение
Identifies tatpuruṣa compound. Sāmāsika = compound formation. Yoga-vidhi = "union-method/procedure" (karmadhāraya or tatpuruṣa type).
8
vi ā iti dve pade
Translation
Vi and ā [in vyākhyāsyāmaḥ] [are] two (dve) words (pade).
Vi and ā (in vyākhyāsyāmaḥ) are two words.
Словарь
• vi = prefix vi- (separate)
• ā = prefix ā- (toward)
• iti = thus
• dve = two (nom. dual n.)
• pade = words (nom. dual n.)
Объяснение
Prefix analysis. Treats verbal prefixes (upasargas) as separate countable units. Vi = "apart, distinctly"; ā = "toward, thoroughly". Together modify root √khyā "to tell."
9
khyāsyāma ity ākhyātikam
Translation
Khyāsyāmaḥ [is] the verb (ākhyātikam).
Khyāsyāmaḥ is the verb.
Словарь
• khyāsyāmaḥ = we shall tell/explain (1st pl. fut.)
• iti = thus
• ākhyātikam = verbal form, finite verb (nom. sg. n.)
Объяснение
Identifies the finite verbal element (ākhyāta). Root √khyā + future tense + 1st person plural = vyākhyāsyāmaḥ "we shall explain fully."

5
yasmād evam hy āha:
yathā vivṛtagātro 'pi śirasi prāvṛto naraḥ |
nābhivyaktim vrajaty evam sūtram vigrahavarjitam ||
Translation
Hence (yasmāt) the following has indeed (hi) been said (āha): "Just as (yathā) a man (naraḥ) whose head (śirasi) is veiled (prāvṛtaḥ) remains obscure, even (api) if his limbs (gātraḥ) are unveiled (vivṛta), so (evam) does a Sūtra (sūtram) without (varjitam) a word division (vigraha)."
Hence the following has indeed rightly been said: "Just as a man whose head is veiled remains obscure, even if his limbs are unveiled, so does a Sūtra without a (word) division."
Словарь
• yasmāt = because
• evam = thus
• hi = indeed
• āha = [someone] said
• yathā = just as
• vivṛta-gātraḥ = with unveiled limbs
• api = even though
• śirasi = on the head (loc.)
• prāvṛtaḥ = veiled, covered
• naraḥ = man
• na = not
• abhivyaktim = manifestation, clarity
• vrajati = goes, attains
• evam = so, thus
• sūtram = sūtra
• vigraha-varjitam = without word-division
Объяснение
Scriptural analogy: even with clear parts, if the head (main point) is veiled, nothing is clear. Similarly, without word-analysis, even a sūtra's meaning stays hidden.
6
