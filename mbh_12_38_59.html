Mahābhārata — Śāntiparvan 12.38 & 12.59

Reading text (Sanskrit verbatim, GRETIL/Tokunaga) + English translation. The two chunks we compared: 12.38 (Yudhiṣṭhira’s question on dharma + Bhīṣma’s teachers, Uśanas & Brahmā’s son) and 12.59 (origin of kingship & the decline of dharma).

Mahābhārata 12.38.1–17

Yudhiṣṭhira asks to hear the rājadharma; Vyāsa sends him to Bhīṣma, naming his teachers — Uśanas (38.10) and the son of Brahmā, Sanatkumāra (38.12).
38.1
śrotum icchāmi bhagavan vistareṇa mahāmune
Yudhiṣṭhira said: I wish to hear in full, O blessed great sage, the duties of kings (rājadharma), best of the twice-born, and all those of the four classes (cāturvarṇya), and how a ruler should frame policy (nīti) in times of distress (āpatsu) —
38.2
rājadharmān dvijaśreṣṭha cāturvarṇyasya cākhilān āpatsu ca yathā nītir vidhātavyā mahīkṣitā
and how, treading the path of dharma (dharmyaṃ panthānam), I might win the earth.
38.3
dharmyam ālambya panthānaṃ vijayeyaṃ kathaṃ mahīm prāyaścittakathā hy eṣā bhakṣyābhakṣyavivardhitā
For this is really a discourse on expiation (prāyaścitta-kathā), swollen with questions of the edible and inedible;
38.4
kautūhalānupravaṇā harṣaṃ janayatīva me dharmacaryā ca rājyaṃ ca nityam eva virudhyate
bent on curiosity, it stirs a kind of gladness in me. Yet the practice of dharma (dharmacaryā) and kingship (rājya) are forever in conflict (virudhyate).
38.5
tam uvāca mahātejā vyāso vedavidāṃ varaḥ
Vaiśaṃpāyana said: To him the mighty Vyāsa, best of the knowers of the Veda,
38.6
nāradaṃ samabhiprekṣya sarvaṃ jānan purātanam śrotum icchasi ced dharmān akhilena yudhiṣṭhira
looking toward Nārada and knowing all that is ancient (purātanam), said: If you wish to hear the dharmas in full, Yudhiṣṭhira,
38.7
praihi bhīṣmaṃ mahābāho vṛddhaṃ kurupitāmaham sa te sarvarahasyeṣu saṃśayān manasi sthitān
go to Bhīṣma (praihi bhīṣmam), the aged grandsire of the Kurus (kuru-pitāmaham); in all secret matters he will cut away (chettā) the doubts lodged in your mind —
38.8
chettā bhāgīrathīputraḥ sarvajñaḥ sarvadharmavit janayām āsa yaṃ devī divyā tripathagā nadī
the son of Bhāgīrathī, the all-knower, knower of every dharma, whom the divine three-pathed river (Gaṅgā) bore (janayām āsa);
38.9
sākṣād dadarśa yo devān sarvāñ śakrapurogamān bṛhaspatipurogāṃś ca devarṣīn asakṛt prabhuḥ
who directly beheld the gods, all led by Śakra, and the divine seers led by Bṛhaspati, again and again — the mighty one;
38.10
toṣayitvopacāreṇa rājanītim adhītavān uśanā veda yac chāstraṃ devāsuragurur dvijaḥ
having gratified [them] with service, he studied statecraft (rājanīti); and the śāstra that Uśanas (uśanā) knows — that twice-born preceptor of gods and asuras —
38.11
tac ca sarvaṃ savaiyākhyaṃ prāptavān kurusattamaḥ bhārgavāc cyavanāc cāpi vedān aṅgopabṛṃhitān
all of it, with its glosses, the best of Kurus obtained; and from Bhārgava and Cyavana too [he gained] the Vedas augmented by the limbs (aṅga);
38.12
pratipede mahābuddhir vasiṣṭhāc ca yatavratāt pitāmahasutaṃ jyeṣṭhaṃ kumāraṃ dīptatejasam
the greatly wise one received [teaching] from Vasiṣṭha of firm vows, and from the eldest son of the Grandsire (pitāmaha-sutaṃ jyeṣṭhaṃ kumāram = Sanatkumāra), the youth of blazing energy,
38.13
adhyātmagatitattvajñam upāśikṣata yaḥ purā mārkaṇḍeyamukhāt kṛtsnaṃ yatidharmam avāptavān
who knows the truth of the way of the inner self (adhyātma), under whom he studied of old; from the mouth of Mārkaṇḍeya he gained the whole dharma of ascetics (yatidharma);
38.14
rāmād astrāṇi śakrāc ca prāptavān bharatarṣabha mṛtyur ātmecchayā yasya jātasya manujeṣv api
from Rāma (Paraśurāma) [he gained] weapons, and from Śakra — he upon whose birth, even among men, death waits at his own wish (mṛtyur ātmecchayā);
38.15
tathānapatyasya sataḥ puṇyalokā divi śrutāḥ yasya brahmarṣayaḥ puṇyā nityam āsan sabhāsadaḥ
and though childless, holy worlds in heaven are said to be his; in whose assembly holy brahmin-seers (brahmarṣayaḥ) were ever seated;
38.16
yasya nāviditaṃ kiṃ cij jñānajñeyeṣu vidyate sa te vakṣyati dharmajñaḥ sūkṣmadharmārthatattvavit
to whom nothing among things-to-be-known is unknown — he, knower of dharma, knower of the subtle truth of dharma and artha, will declare it to you.
38.17
tam abhyehi purā prāṇān sa vimuñcati dharmavit evam uktas tu kaunteyo dīrghaprajño mahādyutiḥ
Approach him before he releases his vital breaths (prāṇān vimuñcati), the knower of dharma.

Mahābhārata 12.59.1–30

“How did kingship arise?” — Bhīṣma narrates the decline (kheda → moha → lobha → kāma → rāga → the perishing of brahman & dharma) and the gods’ appeal to Brahmā.
59.1
tataḥ kālyaṃ samutthāya kṛtapaurvāhṇikakriyāḥ
Vaiśaṃpāyana said: Then, rising at dawn (kālyaṃ samutthāya), having performed the morning rites,
59.2
yayus te nagarākārai rathaiḥ pāṇḍavayādavāḥ prapadya ca kurukṣetraṃ bhīṣmam āsādya cānagham
the Pāṇḍavas and Yādavas went on chariots [tall] as city-gates and, reaching Kurukṣetra, came up to the sinless Bhīṣma;
59.3
sukhāṃ ca rajanīṃ pṛṣṭvā gāṅgeyaṃ rathināṃ varam vyāsādīn abhivādyarṣīn sarvais taiś cābhinanditāḥ
having asked the Gaṅgā-son, best of chariot-warriors, about his comfortable night, and saluting the seers led by Vyāsa, welcomed by them all,
59.4
niṣedur abhito bhīṣmaṃ parivārya samantataḥ tato rājā mahātejā dharmarājo yudhiṣṭhiraḥ
they sat down around Bhīṣma on every side. Then the great king Yudhiṣṭhira, the Dharma-king,
59.5
abravīt prāñjalir bhīṣmaṃ pratipūjyābhivādya ca ya eṣa rājā-rājeti śabdaś carati bhārata
with joined palms, having honoured and saluted Bhīṣma, said: This word that goes about, “king, king” (rājā-rājā), O Bhārata —
59.6
katham eṣa samutpannas tan me brūhi pitāmaha tulyapāṇiśirogrīvas tulyabuddhīndriyātmakaḥ
how did this one arise? Tell me, grandsire (pitāmaha). He has hands, head and neck like [others], like in mind and senses,
59.7
tulyaduḥkhasukhātmā ca tulyapṛṣṭhabhujodaraḥ tulyaśukrāsthimajjaś ca tulyamāṃsāsṛg eva ca
alike in pain and pleasure, alike in back, arms and belly, alike in semen, bone and marrow, alike in flesh and blood,
59.8
niḥśvāsocchvāsatulyaś ca tulyaprāṇaśarīravān samānajanmamaraṇaḥ samaḥ sarvaguṇair nṛṇām
alike in out-breath and in-breath, alike in life and body, the same in birth and death, equal to [other] men in every quality —
59.9
viśiṣṭabuddhīñ śūrāṃś ca katham eko 'dhitiṣṭhati katham eko mahīṃ kṛtsnāṃ vīraśūrāryasaṃkulām
how does one [man] stand over the [more] intelligent and the brave? How does one guard the whole earth, thronged with heroes, warriors and nobles?
59.10
rakṣaty api ca loko 'sya prasādam abhivāñchati ekasya ca prasādena kṛtsno lokaḥ prasīdati
The whole world seeks his favour (prasāda); by the favour of one, the whole world is gladdened (prasīdati).
59.11
vyākulenākulaḥ sarvo bhavatīti viniścayaḥ etad icchāmy ahaṃ sarvaṃ tattvena bharatarṣabha
When he is troubled, all become troubled — so it is settled. All this I wish [to know], truly, O bull of the Bharatas;
59.12
śrotuṃ tan me yathātattvaṃ prabrūhi vadatāṃ vara naitat kāraṇam alpaṃ hi bhaviṣyati viśāṃ pate
tell it to me as it truly is, best of speakers; for the cause of this cannot be slight, O lord of peoples.
59.13
niyatas tvaṃ naraśreṣṭha śṛṇu sarvam aśeṣataḥ
Bhīṣma said: Listen, best of men, with full attention, to all without remainder —
59.14
yathā rājyaṃ samutpannam ādau kṛtayuge 'bhavat naiva rājyaṃ na rājāsīn na daṇḍo na ca dāṇḍikaḥ
how kingship arose, as it was in the beginning in the Kṛta age. There was neither kingship nor king, neither rod (daṇḍa) nor punisher;
59.15
dharmeṇaiva prajāḥ sarvā rakṣanti ca parasparam pālayānās tathānyonyaṃ narā dharmeṇa bhārata
by dharma alone all subjects protected one another. But protecting one another thus by dharma, O Bhārata,
59.16
khedaṃ paramam ājagmus tatas tān moha āviśat te mohavaśam āpannā mānavā manujarṣabha
they came to utter weariness (kheda); then delusion (moha) entered them. Fallen under delusion’s sway, O bull among men,
59.17
pratipattivimohāc ca dharmas teṣām anīnaśat naṣṭāyāṃ pratipattau tu mohavaśyā narās tadā
through the confounding of judgment (pratipatti-vimoha) their dharma perished. When judgment was lost, the men, mastered by delusion,
59.18
lobhasya vaśam āpannāḥ sarve bhāratasattama aprāptasyābhimarśaṃ tu kurvanto manujās tataḥ
all fell under the sway of greed (lobha), O best of Bharatas. Then, as men laid hold of what was not [rightly] theirs (aprāptasya abhimarśam),
59.19
kāmo nāmāparas tatra samapadyata vai prabho tāṃs tu kāmavaśaṃ prāptān rāgo nāma samaspṛśat
another thing called desire (kāma) arose, O lord; and as they came under desire’s sway, a thing called passion (rāga) touched them.
59.20
raktāś ca nābhyajānanta kāryākāryaṃ yudhiṣṭhira agamyāgamanaṃ caiva vācyāvācyaṃ tathaiva ca
Impassioned, they no longer knew the to-be-done from the not-to-be-done (kāryākārya), O Yudhiṣṭhira; the approachable and unapproachable (agamyāgamana), the speakable and unspeakable likewise,
59.21
bhakṣyābhakṣyaṃ ca rājendra doṣādoṣaṃ ca nātyajan viplute naraloke 'smiṃs tato brahma nanāśa ha
the edible and inedible (bhakṣyābhakṣya), O king of kings, the faulty and faultless — they no longer shunned. When this world of men was overwhelmed (viplute naraloke), then brahman perished —
59.22
nāśāc ca brahmaṇo rājan dharmo nāśam athāgamat naṣṭe brahmaṇi dharme ca devās trāsam athāgaman
and from the perishing of brahman, O king, dharma too came to ruin. When brahman and dharma had perished, the gods were seized with fear.
59.23
te trastā naraśārdūla brahmāṇaṃ śaraṇaṃ yayuḥ prapadya bhagavantaṃ te devā lokapitāmaham
Terrified, O tiger among men, they went to Brahmā for refuge; approaching the blessed Lord, the Grandsire of the worlds (loka-pitāmaha),
59.24
ūcuḥ prāñjalayaḥ sarve duḥkhaśokabhayārditāḥ bhagavan naralokasthaṃ naṣṭaṃ brahma sanātanam
with joined palms, all afflicted by sorrow, grief and fear, they said: O blessed one, the eternal brahman that dwelt in the world of men has perished;
59.25
lobhamohādibhir bhāvais tato no bhayam āviśat brahmaṇaś ca praṇāśena dharmo 'py anaśad īśvara
through greed, delusion and the rest, fear has therefore entered us; and by the destruction of brahman, dharma too has perished, O Lord.
59.26
tataḥ sma samatāṃ yātā martyais tribhuvaneśvara adho hi varṣam asmākaṃ martyās tūrdhvapravarṣiṇaḥ
So we have come to a level with mortals, O lord of the three worlds; for our realm is below, while the mortals above rain up [their offerings] (ūrdhva-pravarṣiṇaḥ).
59.27
kriyāvyuparamāt teṣāṃ tato 'gacchāma saṃśayam atra niḥśreyasaṃ yan nas tad dhyāyasva pitāmaha
Because their rites have ceased (kriyā-vyuparamāt) we have come to peril; reflect, grandsire, on what is the highest good (niḥśreyasa) for us in this.
59.28
tvatprabhāvasamuttho 'sau prabhāvo no vinaśyati tān uvāca surān sarvān svayaṃbhūr bhagavāṃs tataḥ
This power of ours, sprung from your power (tvat-prabhāva), is perishing. Then the Self-existent (svayaṃbhū), the blessed one, said to all the gods:
59.29
śreyo 'haṃ cintayiṣyāmi vyetu vo bhīḥ surarṣabhāḥ tato 'dhyāyasahasrāṇāṃ śataṃ cakre svabuddhijam
I will think upon [your] welfare; let your fear depart, O bulls among gods. Then from his own intellect he made a hundred thousand chapters,
59.30
yatra dharmas tathaivārthaḥ kāmaś caivānuvarṇitaḥ trivarga iti vikhyāto gaṇa eṣa svayaṃbhuvā
in which dharma, and likewise artha and kāma were set forth — this collection (gaṇa), famed as the “triple set” (trivarga), [made] by the Self-existent.
