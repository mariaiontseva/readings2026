Mahābhārata — Śāntiparvan 12.38 & 12.59

Verses in pādas (Sanskrit verbatim, GRETIL/Tokunaga) with word-by-word translation — the Sanskrit of each word in (parentheses).

Mahābhārata 12.38.1–17

Yudhiṣṭhira asks to hear the rājadharma; Vyāsa sends him to Bhīṣma, naming his teachers — Uśanas (38.10) and the son of Brahmā, Sanatkumāra (38.12).
38.1
śrotum icchāmi bhagavan vistareṇa mahāmune |
I wish (icchāmi) to hear (śrotum), O blessed one (bhagavan), in detail (vistareṇa), O great sage (mahāmune) —
38.2
rājadharmān dvijaśreṣṭha cāturvarṇyasya cākhilān |
āpatsu ca yathā nītir vidhātavyā mahīkṣitā ||
the kings’ duties (rājadharmān), O best of the twice-born (dvijaśreṣṭha), and all (akhilān) those of the four classes (cāturvarṇyasya ca), and how (yathā) policy (nītiḥ) is to be applied (vidhātavyā) by a ruler (mahīkṣitā) in calamities (āpatsu).
38.3
dharmyam ālambya panthānaṃ vijayeyaṃ kathaṃ mahīm |
prāyaścittakathā hy eṣā bhakṣyābhakṣyavivardhitā ||
Holding to (ālambya) the path (panthānam) of dharma (dharmyam), how (katham) may I win (vijayeyam) the earth (mahīm)? For (hi) this (eṣā) is a discourse on expiation (prāyaścitta-kathā), swollen with (vivardhitā) [matters of] edible-and-inedible (bhakṣyābhakṣya);
38.4
kautūhalānupravaṇā harṣaṃ janayatīva me |
dharmacaryā ca rājyaṃ ca nityam eva virudhyate ||
inclined to curiosity (kautūhala-anupravaṇā), it generates (janayati iva) in me (me) a kind of joy (harṣam); [yet] the practice of dharma (dharmacaryā ca) and kingship (rājyam ca) are ever (nityam eva) at odds (virudhyate).
38.5
tam uvāca mahātejā vyāso vedavidāṃ varaḥ |
(Vaiśaṃpāyana said:) To him (tam) spoke (uvāca) the greatly-splendid (mahātejāḥ) Vyāsa (vyāsaḥ), best (varaḥ) of the knowers of the Veda (vedavidām),
38.6
nāradaṃ samabhiprekṣya sarvaṃ jānan purātanam |
śrotum icchasi ced dharmān akhilena yudhiṣṭhira ||
looking toward (samabhiprekṣya) Nārada (nāradam), knowing (jānan) all (sarvam) that is ancient (purātanam): If (ced) you wish (icchasi) to hear (śrotum) the dharmas (dharmān) in full (akhilena), Yudhiṣṭhira —
38.7
praihi bhīṣmaṃ mahābāho vṛddhaṃ kurupitāmaham |
sa te sarvarahasyeṣu saṃśayān manasi sthitān ||
go to (praihi) Bhīṣma (bhīṣmam), O mighty-armed (mahābāho), the aged (vṛddham) grandsire of the Kurus (kuru-pitāmaham); he (saḥ) [will clear] your (te) doubts (saṃśayān) lodged (sthitān) in the mind (manasi) about all secret matters (sarva-rahasyeṣu) —
38.8
chettā bhāgīrathīputraḥ sarvajñaḥ sarvadharmavit |
janayām āsa yaṃ devī divyā tripathagā nadī ||
he will cut [them] away (chettā), the son of Bhāgīrathī (bhāgīrathī-putraḥ), all-knowing (sarvajñaḥ), knower of every dharma (sarva-dharma-vit), whom (yam) the divine (divyā), three-pathed (tripathagā) river-goddess (devī … nadī) bore (janayām āsa);
38.9
sākṣād dadarśa yo devān sarvāñ śakrapurogamān |
bṛhaspatipurogāṃś ca devarṣīn asakṛt prabhuḥ ||
who (yaḥ) directly (sākṣāt) beheld (dadarśa) the gods (devān), all (sarvān) led by Śakra (śakra-purogamān), and the divine seers (devarṣīn) led by Bṛhaspati (bṛhaspati-purogān), again and again (asakṛt) — the mighty one (prabhuḥ);
38.10
toṣayitvopacāreṇa rājanītim adhītavān |
uśanā veda yac chāstraṃ devāsuragurur dvijaḥ ||
having gratified [them] (toṣayitvā) with service (upacāreṇa), he studied (adhītavān) statecraft (rājanītim); the śāstra (śāstram) that (yat) Uśanas (uśanā) knows (veda) — that twice-born one (dvijaḥ), preceptor of gods and asuras (deva-asura-guruḥ) —
38.11
tac ca sarvaṃ savaiyākhyaṃ prāptavān kurusattamaḥ |
bhārgavāc cyavanāc cāpi vedān aṅgopabṛṃhitān ||
all (sarvam) of that (tat), with its glosses (sa-vaiyākhyam), the best of Kurus (kuru-sattamaḥ) obtained (prāptavān); and from Bhārgava (bhārgavāt) and from Cyavana too (cyavanāt ca api) [he gained] the Vedas (vedān) swelled by the limbs (aṅga-upabṛṃhitān);
38.12
pratipede mahābuddhir vasiṣṭhāc ca yatavratāt |
pitāmahasutaṃ jyeṣṭhaṃ kumāraṃ dīptatejasam ||
the greatly-wise one (mahābuddhiḥ) received [teaching] (pratipede) from Vasiṣṭha too (vasiṣṭhāt ca) of firm vows (yata-vratāt), and [from] the firstborn (jyeṣṭham) son of the Grandsire (pitāmaha-sutam), the youth (kumāram) of blazing energy (dīpta-tejasam),
38.13
adhyātmagatitattvajñam upāśikṣata yaḥ purā |
mārkaṇḍeyamukhāt kṛtsnaṃ yatidharmam avāptavān ||
who knows the truth of the inner-self’s course (adhyātma-gati-tattvajñam), under whom (yaḥ) [he] studied (upāśikṣata) of old (purā); from the mouth of Mārkaṇḍeya (mārkaṇḍeya-mukhāt) he obtained (avāptavān) the whole (kṛtsnam) ascetics’ dharma (yati-dharmam);
38.14
rāmād astrāṇi śakrāc ca prāptavān bharatarṣabha |
mṛtyur ātmecchayā yasya jātasya manujeṣv api ||
from Rāma (rāmāt) [he got] weapons (astrāṇi) and from Śakra (śakrāt ca) — he obtained [them] (prāptavān), O bull of the Bharatas (bharatarṣabha); for whom (yasya), once born (jātasya), death (mṛtyuḥ) [comes] at his own wish (ātma-icchayā) even (api) among men (manujeṣu);
38.15
tathānapatyasya sataḥ puṇyalokā divi śrutāḥ |
yasya brahmarṣayaḥ puṇyā nityam āsan sabhāsadaḥ ||
and (tathā), though childless (anapatyasya sataḥ), holy worlds (puṇya-lokāḥ) in heaven (divi) are heard of [as his] (śrutāḥ); in whose [hall] (yasya) holy (puṇyāḥ) brahmin-seers (brahmarṣayaḥ) were ever (nityam āsan) court-members (sabhā-sadaḥ);
38.16
yasya nāviditaṃ kiṃ cij jñānajñeyeṣu vidyate |
sa te vakṣyati dharmajñaḥ sūkṣmadharmārthatattvavit ||
in whom (yasya) nothing (na kiṃcit) among knowables (jñāna-jñeyeṣu) is (vidyate) unknown (aviditam); he (saḥ), the dharma-knower (dharmajñaḥ), knower of the subtle truth of dharma and artha (sūkṣma-dharma-artha-tattvavit), will tell (vakṣyati) [it] to you (te).
38.17
tam abhyehi purā prāṇān sa vimuñcati dharmavit |
evam uktas tu kaunteyo dīrghaprajño mahādyutiḥ ||
Approach (abhyehi) him (tam) before (purā) he (saḥ), the dharma-knower (dharmavit), releases (vimuñcati) [his] breaths (prāṇān). — Thus addressed (evam uktaḥ), the son of Kuntī (kaunteyaḥ), far-sighted (dīrgha-prajñaḥ), of great brilliance (mahādyutiḥ) …

Mahābhārata 12.59.1–30

“How did kingship arise?” — Bhīṣma narrates the decline (kheda → moha → lobha → kāma → rāga → the perishing of brahman & dharma) and the gods’ appeal to Brahmā.
59.1
tataḥ kālyaṃ samutthāya kṛtapaurvāhṇikakriyāḥ |
(Vaiśaṃpāyana said:) Then (tataḥ), rising (samutthāya) at daybreak (kālyam), having done the morning rites (kṛta-paurvāhṇika-kriyāḥ),
59.2
yayus te nagarākārai rathaiḥ pāṇḍavayādavāḥ |
prapadya ca kurukṣetraṃ bhīṣmam āsādya cānagham ||
they went (yayuḥ), the Pāṇḍavas-and-Yādavas (pāṇḍava-yādavāḥ), on chariots (rathaiḥ) like city-gates (nagara-ākāraiḥ); and reaching (prapadya) Kurukṣetra (kurukṣetram), having come up to (āsādya) the sinless (anagham) Bhīṣma (bhīṣmam),
59.3
sukhāṃ ca rajanīṃ pṛṣṭvā gāṅgeyaṃ rathināṃ varam |
vyāsādīn abhivādyarṣīn sarvais taiś cābhinanditāḥ ||
having asked (pṛṣṭvā) the Gaṅgā-son (gāṅgeyam), best of charioteers (rathinām varam), about his comfortable (sukhām) night (rajanīm), and saluting (abhivādya) the seers (ṛṣīn) led by Vyāsa (vyāsa-ādīn), welcomed (abhinanditāḥ) by them all (sarvaiḥ taiḥ ca),
59.4
niṣedur abhito bhīṣmaṃ parivārya samantataḥ |
tato rājā mahātejā dharmarājo yudhiṣṭhiraḥ ||
they sat down (niṣeduḥ) close around (abhitaḥ) Bhīṣma (bhīṣmam), surrounding [him] (parivārya) on every side (samantataḥ). Then (tataḥ) the king (rājā) of great splendour (mahātejāḥ), the Dharma-king (dharmarājaḥ) Yudhiṣṭhira (yudhiṣṭhiraḥ),
59.5
abravīt prāñjalir bhīṣmaṃ pratipūjyābhivādya ca |
ya eṣa rājā-rājeti śabdaś carati bhārata ||
with joined palms (prāñjaliḥ), having honoured and saluted (pratipūjya abhivādya ca) Bhīṣma (bhīṣmam), said (abravīt): This (eṣaḥ) sound “king, king” (rājā-rājā iti śabdaḥ) that (yaḥ) goes about (carati), O Bhārata —
59.6
katham eṣa samutpannas tan me brūhi pitāmaha |
tulyapāṇiśirogrīvas tulyabuddhīndriyātmakaḥ ||
how (katham) did this one (eṣaḥ) arise (samutpannaḥ)? Tell (brūhi) me (me) that (tat), grandsire (pitāmaha). [He is] equal in hands, head, neck (tulya-pāṇi-śiro-grīvaḥ), equal in mind and senses (tulya-buddhi-indriya-ātmakaḥ),
59.7
tulyaduḥkhasukhātmā ca tulyapṛṣṭhabhujodaraḥ |
tulyaśukrāsthimajjaś ca tulyamāṃsāsṛg eva ca ||
alike in pain and pleasure (tulya-duḥkha-sukha-ātmā ca), alike in back, arms, belly (tulya-pṛṣṭha-bhuja-udaraḥ), alike in semen, bone, marrow (tulya-śukra-asthi-majjaḥ ca), alike in flesh and blood too (tulya-māṃsa-asṛk eva ca),
59.8
niḥśvāsocchvāsatulyaś ca tulyaprāṇaśarīravān |
samānajanmamaraṇaḥ samaḥ sarvaguṇair nṛṇām ||
alike in out- and in-breath (niḥśvāsa-ucchvāsa-tulyaḥ ca), having like life and body (tulya-prāṇa-śarīravān), the same in birth and death (samāna-janma-maraṇaḥ), equal (samaḥ) to men (nṛṇām) in all qualities (sarva-guṇaiḥ) —
59.9
viśiṣṭabuddhīñ śūrāṃś ca katham eko 'dhitiṣṭhati |
katham eko mahīṃ kṛtsnāṃ vīraśūrāryasaṃkulām ||
how (katham) does one [man] (ekaḥ) stand over (adhitiṣṭhati) the more intelligent (viśiṣṭa-buddhīn) and the brave (śūrān ca)? How (katham) [does] one (ekaḥ) [guard] the whole (kṛtsnām) earth (mahīm), thronged with heroes, warriors, nobles (vīra-śūra-ārya-saṃkulām),
59.10
rakṣaty api ca loko 'sya prasādam abhivāñchati |
ekasya ca prasādena kṛtsno lokaḥ prasīdati ||
and (api ca) [the world] (lokaḥ) guards (rakṣati) it (asya) and seeks (abhivāñchati) his favour (prasādam); by the favour (prasādena) of one (ekasya) the whole (kṛtsnaḥ) world (lokaḥ) is gladdened (prasīdati)?
59.11
vyākulenākulaḥ sarvo bhavatīti viniścayaḥ |
etad icchāmy ahaṃ sarvaṃ tattvena bharatarṣabha ||
When [he is] troubled (vyākulena), everyone (sarvaḥ) becomes (bhavati) troubled (ākulaḥ) — such (iti) is the certainty (viniścayaḥ). All (sarvam) this (etat) I (aham) wish [to know] (icchāmi) truly (tattvena), O bull of the Bharatas (bharatarṣabha);
59.12
śrotuṃ tan me yathātattvaṃ prabrūhi vadatāṃ vara |
naitat kāraṇam alpaṃ hi bhaviṣyati viśāṃ pate ||
to hear (śrotum) it (tat) — for me (me) — as it truly is (yathā-tattvam): declare [it] (prabrūhi), best of speakers (vadatām vara); for (hi) this (etat) cause (kāraṇam) will (bhaviṣyati) not (na) be slight (alpam), O lord of peoples (viśām pate).
59.13
niyatas tvaṃ naraśreṣṭha śṛṇu sarvam aśeṣataḥ |
(Bhīṣma said:) Attentive (niyataḥ) [be] you (tvam), O best of men (naraśreṣṭha) — listen (śṛṇu) to all (sarvam) without remainder (aśeṣataḥ):
59.14
yathā rājyaṃ samutpannam ādau kṛtayuge 'bhavat |
naiva rājyaṃ na rājāsīn na daṇḍo na ca dāṇḍikaḥ ||
how (yathā) kingship (rājyam) arose (samutpannam), as it was (abhavat) in the beginning (ādau) in the Kṛta age (kṛtayuge): there was neither (na eva) kingship (rājyam) nor (na) king (rājā āsīt), nor (na) rod (daṇḍaḥ) nor (na ca) punisher (dāṇḍikaḥ);
59.15
dharmeṇaiva prajāḥ sarvā rakṣanti ca parasparam |
pālayānās tathānyonyaṃ narā dharmeṇa bhārata ||
by dharma alone (dharmeṇa eva) all (sarvāḥ) the subjects (prajāḥ) protected (rakṣanti ca) one another (parasparam); protecting (pālayānāḥ) one another (anyonyam) thus (tathā) by dharma (dharmeṇa), O Bhārata,
59.16
khedaṃ paramam ājagmus tatas tān moha āviśat |
te mohavaśam āpannā mānavā manujarṣabha ||
they came (ājagmuḥ) to utmost (paramam) weariness (khedam); then (tataḥ) delusion (mohaḥ) entered (āviśat) them (tān). Fallen (āpannāḥ) under delusion’s sway (moha-vaśam), the men (mānavāḥ), O bull among men (manujarṣabha),
59.17
pratipattivimohāc ca dharmas teṣām anīnaśat |
naṣṭāyāṃ pratipattau tu mohavaśyā narās tadā ||
and (ca) through the confounding of judgment (pratipatti-vimohāt) their (teṣām) dharma (dharmaḥ) perished (anīnaśat). When judgment was lost (naṣṭāyāṃ pratipattau tu), the men (narāḥ) then (tadā), mastered by delusion (moha-vaśyāḥ),
59.18
lobhasya vaśam āpannāḥ sarve bhāratasattama |
aprāptasyābhimarśaṃ tu kurvanto manujās tataḥ ||
all (sarve) fell (āpannāḥ) under greed’s sway (lobhasya vaśam), O best of Bharatas (bhāratasattama); then (tataḥ), as the men (manujāḥ) made seizure (abhimarśaṃ kurvantaḥ) of what was not their own (aprāptasya),
59.19
kāmo nāmāparas tatra samapadyata vai prabho |
tāṃs tu kāmavaśaṃ prāptān rāgo nāma samaspṛśat ||
another thing (aparaḥ) called desire (kāmaḥ nāma) arose (samapadyata vai) there (tatra), O lord (prabho); and those (tān) fallen under desire’s sway (kāma-vaśaṃ prāptān) — a thing called passion (rāgaḥ nāma) touched (samaspṛśat).
59.20
raktāś ca nābhyajānanta kāryākāryaṃ yudhiṣṭhira |
agamyāgamanaṃ caiva vācyāvācyaṃ tathaiva ca ||
And impassioned (raktāḥ ca) they no longer knew (na abhyajānanta) to-do-and-not-to-do (kāryākāryam), O Yudhiṣṭhira; the approachable-and-unapproachable [woman] (agamya-āgamanam ca eva), the speakable-and-unspeakable (vācya-avācyam) likewise (tathā eva ca),
59.21
bhakṣyābhakṣyaṃ ca rājendra doṣādoṣaṃ ca nātyajan |
viplute naraloke 'smiṃs tato brahma nanāśa ha ||
the edible-and-inedible (bhakṣya-abhakṣyam ca), O king of kings (rājendra), the faulty-and-faultless (doṣa-adoṣam ca) — they did not shun (na atyajan). When this (asmin) world of men (naraloke) was overwhelmed (viplute), then (tataḥ) brahman (brahma) perished indeed (nanāśa ha);
59.22
nāśāc ca brahmaṇo rājan dharmo nāśam athāgamat |
naṣṭe brahmaṇi dharme ca devās trāsam athāgaman ||
and from the perishing (nāśāt ca) of brahman (brahmaṇaḥ), O king (rājan), dharma (dharmaḥ) too came (athāgamat) to destruction (nāśam); when brahman and dharma had perished (naṣṭe brahmaṇi dharme ca), the gods (devāḥ) fell into terror (trāsam athāgaman).
59.23
te trastā naraśārdūla brahmāṇaṃ śaraṇaṃ yayuḥ |
prapadya bhagavantaṃ te devā lokapitāmaham ||
Terrified (trastāḥ), O tiger among men (naraśārdūla), they (te) went (yayuḥ) to Brahmā (brahmāṇam) for refuge (śaraṇam); approaching (prapadya) the blessed one (bhagavantam), the gods (devāḥ) [went to] the Grandsire of the worlds (loka-pitāmaham),
59.24
ūcuḥ prāñjalayaḥ sarve duḥkhaśokabhayārditāḥ |
bhagavan naralokasthaṃ naṣṭaṃ brahma sanātanam ||
[and] with joined palms (prāñjalayaḥ), all (sarve) afflicted by pain, grief, fear (duḥkha-śoka-bhaya-arditāḥ), they said (ūcuḥ): O blessed one (bhagavan), the eternal (sanātanam) brahman (brahma) that dwelt in the world of men (naraloka-stham) has perished (naṣṭam);
59.25
lobhamohādibhir bhāvais tato no bhayam āviśat |
brahmaṇaś ca praṇāśena dharmo 'py anaśad īśvara ||
through greed, delusion and the rest (lobha-moha-ādibhiḥ bhāvaiḥ) fear (bhayam) has therefore (tataḥ) entered (āviśat) us (naḥ); and by the destruction (praṇāśena) of brahman (brahmaṇaḥ ca) dharma too (dharmaḥ api) has perished (anaśat), O Lord (īśvara).
59.26
tataḥ sma samatāṃ yātā martyais tribhuvaneśvara |
adho hi varṣam asmākaṃ martyās tūrdhvapravarṣiṇaḥ ||
Thereby (tataḥ sma) we have come (yātāḥ) to a level (samatām) with mortals (martyaiḥ), O lord of the three worlds (tribhuvaneśvara); for (hi) our (asmākam) realm (varṣam) is below (adhaḥ), while the mortals (martyāḥ) [are] above, raining upward (ūrdhva-pravarṣiṇaḥ).
59.27
kriyāvyuparamāt teṣāṃ tato 'gacchāma saṃśayam |
atra niḥśreyasaṃ yan nas tad dhyāyasva pitāmaha ||
Because their rites have ceased (kriyā-vyuparamāt teṣām) we have therefore (tataḥ) come (agacchāma) to peril (saṃśayam); reflect (dhyāyasva), grandsire (pitāmaha), on that (tat) which (yat) [is] our (naḥ) highest good (niḥśreyasam) herein (atra).
59.28
tvatprabhāvasamuttho 'sau prabhāvo no vinaśyati |
tān uvāca surān sarvān svayaṃbhūr bhagavāṃs tataḥ ||
This (asau) power of ours (prabhāvaḥ naḥ), risen from your power (tvat-prabhāva-samutthaḥ), is perishing (vinaśyati). Then (tataḥ) the Self-existent (svayaṃbhūḥ), the blessed one (bhagavān), said (uvāca) to all (sarvān) those gods (tān surān):
59.29
śreyo 'haṃ cintayiṣyāmi vyetu vo bhīḥ surarṣabhāḥ |
tato 'dhyāyasahasrāṇāṃ śataṃ cakre svabuddhijam ||
I (aham) will think upon (cintayiṣyāmi) [your] welfare (śreyaḥ); let your (vaḥ) fear (bhīḥ) depart (vyetu), O bulls among gods (surarṣabhāḥ). Then (tataḥ) he made (cakre) a hundred (śatam) [×] thousands of chapters (adhyāya-sahasrāṇām), sprung from his own mind (sva-buddhi-jam),
59.30
yatra dharmas tathaivārthaḥ kāmaś caivānuvarṇitaḥ |
trivarga iti vikhyāto gaṇa eṣa svayaṃbhuvā ||
in which (yatra) dharma (dharmaḥ), and likewise (tathā eva) artha (arthaḥ), and kāma too (kāmaḥ ca eva) were set forth (anuvarṇitaḥ); this (eṣaḥ) collection (gaṇaḥ), famed (vikhyātaḥ) as (iti) the “triple set” (trivargaḥ) — [made] by the Self-existent (svayaṃbhuvā).

The law of the fishes (mātsyanyāya)

Why a king and the rod are needed: without the daṇḍa “the stronger would roast the weaker like fish on a spit.”
67.16
etasmāt kāraṇād devāḥ prajāpālān pracakrire |
rājā cen na bhavel loke pṛthivyāṃ daṇḍadhārakaḥ ||
For this very reason (etasmāt kāraṇāt) the gods (devāḥ) made (pracakrire) [men] guardians of the people (prajā-pālān): if (ced) there were (bhavet) no (na) king (rājā) in the world (loke), no rod-bearer (daṇḍa-dhārakaḥ) on earth (pṛthivyām) —
67.17
śūle matsyān ivāpakṣyan durbalān balavattarāḥ |
arājakāḥ prajāḥ pūrvaṃ vineśur iti naḥ śrutam ||
the stronger (balavattarāḥ) would roast (apakṣyan) the weaker (durbalān) like fish on a spit (śūle matsyān iva). Kingless (arājakāḥ), the subjects (prajāḥ) of old (pūrvam) perished (vineśuḥ) — so (iti) we have heard (naḥ śrutam);
67.18
parasparaṃ bhakṣayanto matsyā iva jale kṛśān |
devouring (bhakṣayantaḥ) one another (parasparam), like fish (matsyāḥ iva) in water (jale) [devour] the feeble (kṛśān).
15.30
daṇḍaś cen na bhavel loke vyanaśiṣyann imāḥ prajāḥ |
Were there (cen na bhavet) no rod (daṇḍaḥ) in the world (loke), these (imāḥ) subjects (prajāḥ) would utterly perish (vyanaśiṣyan) —
15.31
śūle matsyān ivāpakṣyan durbalān balavattarāḥ |
satyaṃ cedaṃ brahmaṇā pūrvam uktaṃ daṇḍaḥ prajā rakṣati sādhu nītaḥ ||
the stronger (balavattarāḥ) roasting (apakṣyan) the weaker (durbalān) like fish on a spit (śūle matsyān iva). True (satyam) is this (idam), spoken (uktam) of old (pūrvam) by Brahmā (brahmaṇā): the rod (daṇḍaḥ), rightly applied (sādhu nītaḥ), protects (rakṣati) the people (prajāḥ).
